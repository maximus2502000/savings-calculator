About Savings Roast

What this is, how the math works, and why there's a roast involved.

What is this?

Savings Roast is a free calculator that answers one uncomfortable question: how long will your savings actually last?

You enter three numbers — your current savings, your monthly spending, and your monthly income. The calculator tells you your net monthly burn rate, how many months of runway you have left, and the exact month your money runs out if nothing changes.

Then it gives you a spending nickname, a short roast, and three practical tips — because honest feedback lands better when it's at least a little funny.

The whole thing takes 30 seconds. No account. No email required. Your numbers never leave your browser.

How the calculation works

The math is intentionally simple. There are two steps:

Step 1: Monthly Spending − Monthly Income = Burn Rate

This is how much your savings shrink each month.

Step 2: Savings ÷ Burn Rate = Months of Runway

This is how long your savings last at the current rate.

If your income covers your spending (burn rate is zero or negative), your savings are stable. We flag this as a good outcome — your savings are protected at current levels.

We intentionally keep the calculation simple. We do not factor in inflation, investment returns, tax changes, or life events. Those variables matter in long-term planning, but they make the output harder to understand and act on. This tool gives you a clear baseline: if nothing changes, here is what happens. That alone is useful enough to act on.

The three roast levels

The roast is generated based on your financial tier (how many months of runway you have) and the roast level you select. There are three levels:

😌 Gentle

Warm and honest. Like a supportive friend who happens to be an accountant. Acknowledges the situation without alarm.

🌶 Spicy

Sharp and sassy. Sets up a compliment, then pulls the rug. The reader laughs before they wince.

💀 Savage

Dramatic and absurdist. Takes the numbers to their logical extreme. Still punching the situation, never the person.

Every roast follows one rule: it mocks the habit or situation, never the person's worth, intelligence, or circumstances. Nobody chooses to struggle financially. The roast is always punching at behavior — something that can change — not identity.

Who built this?

Savings Roast is an independent project. It was built because most personal finance tools are either intimidatingly complex or aggressively cheerful in a way that avoids the actual numbers.

The goal was something different: a tool that gives you an honest answer quickly, acknowledges that the answer is sometimes uncomfortable, and makes that discomfort slightly more bearable through humor.

If the roast made you laugh or wince (ideally both), it's working as intended.

Not financial advice

Savings Roast is for general estimation and entertainment only. The calculation is simple arithmetic based on the numbers you enter. It does not account for inflation, investment returns, tax implications, changes in income, unexpected expenses, or any other variable that real financial planning involves.

The tips are general guidance, not personalised recommendations. The roast is a joke.

If you are concerned about your financial situation — especially if your runway is very short — please speak to a qualified financial advisor. Many offer free initial consultations, and a real conversation with a professional will always be more useful than a calculator.
